What is a Hackathon?

A hackathon is a collaborative event where students come together to brainstorm, design, and build innovative solutions to real-world challenges within a short time frame. It encourages teamwork, creativity, and practical problem-solving.

Why is it important for CSE students?

  • ⚡ Gain hands-on experience with coding and new technologies.
  • 🤝 Learn teamwork, collaboration, and leadership skills.
  • 💡 Improve problem-solving and logical thinking ability.
  • 🌐 Network with peers, mentors, and industry experts.
  • 🚀 Build projects that can turn into startups or research work.

About The Event

Smart India Hackathon (SIH) is the world's largest open innovation model, launched by the Ministry of Education, Government of India. It brings together students, mentors, government departments, and industry leaders to solve real-world challenges through technology and creativity.

Why SIH is important for Government and Corporate Departments?

Innovative Solutions: Get innovative, cost-effective solutions to real-world problems while giving students the opportunity to contribute to nation building. Corporates can also leverage SIH to strengthen their brand and discover future-ready talent.

Recognition and Visibility: Gain national-level recognition and visibility across premier institutions in India, positioning your department or company as a leader in innovation.

Out-of-the-box Ideas: Tap into the creativity of talented youngsters from all over the country who bring fresh, out-of-the-box perspectives to pressing challenges.

Innovation Movement Opportunity: Be a part of the world's biggest open innovation movement and collaborate with some of the brightest minds in the country to co-create impactful solutions.

Rules & Guidelines – SIH Hackathon

1. Team Formation

Each team must have 6 members with at least 1 female participant. All members should be from the same institute.

2. Original Work

All solutions must be original. Plagiarism or reusing existing projects without credit will lead to disqualification.

3. Time Limit

Projects must be completed within the given hackathon duration. Late submissions will not be evaluated.

4. Submission Guidelines

Final submission must include source code, presentation slides, and a demo video uploaded on the SIH portal.

5. Judging Criteria

Projects will be judged based on Innovation, Feasibility, Impact, Design, and Technical Implementation.

6. Code of Conduct

Maintain professionalism and respect. Any misconduct or violation of SIH guidelines will result in disqualification.
